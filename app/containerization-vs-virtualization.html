Topic Overview

Virtualization vs. Containerization

A comparative analysis based on the study "Virtualization vs. Containerization, a Comparative Approach for Application Deployment in the Computing Continuum Focused on the Edge".

Authors: H. Sturley, A. Fournier, A. Salcedo-Navarro, M. Garcia-Pineda, J. Segura-Garcia

Edge Computing

1. The Core Distinction

The study differentiates the two technologies based on their abstraction layer:

Virtual Machines (VMs)

Virtualize the hardware infrastructure. Each VM runs a full, autonomous Guest OS. They provide precise low-layer control and strong isolation but are resource-heavy.

Containers

Virtualize at the application/OS level. They share the Host OS kernel, making them lightweight, flexible, and portable. They do not virtualize low-layer components like the CPU directly.

2. Evaluation Criteria

The paper compares both methods on three critical pillars for Edge computing:

Compatibility
Based on user experience and ease of installation/deployment.
Scalability
Based on automatic elasticity when facing fluctuating workloads.
Energy Efficiency
Consumption of energy and computer resources (CPU/RAM).

3. Key Findings & Results

Metric Virtual Machines Containers Verdict
Boot Time Slow (Minutes). Requires booting full OS. Fast (Seconds). Processes start instantly. Containers
Resource Usage High overhead (Duplicate OS kernels). Minimal overhead (Shared kernel). Containers
Isolation Strong (Hardware level). Process level (Weaker but improving). VMs
PaaS / SaaS Heavier deployment. Superior performance. Containers
IaaS Standard. Similar performance metrics. Tie

Main Conclusion

The study concludes that containerization is the most ecologically advantageous option in terms of energy consumption, making it the preferred choice for Edge Computing environments where resources are constrained.

4. Why it matters for the Edge?

Edge computing moves data processing closer to the source (IoT devices, gateways) rather than centralized cloud servers.

Constraints

Edge devices often have limited battery, CPU power, and cooling capacity.

Solution

Because containers consume less energy for the same workload, they extend the operational life and efficiency of Edge infrastructure.
